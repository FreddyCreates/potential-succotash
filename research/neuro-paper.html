I. Abstractum (Abstract)

We present NeuroCore, a novel architecture for autonomous distributed systems in which each computational worker possesses two bio-inspired subsystems: a Cor Parvum (Mini Heart) for self-monitoring vital signs and cardiac rhythm regulation, and a Cerebrum Parvum (Mini Brain) for local decision-making via Hebbian learning and attention mechanisms. These subsystems operate within a Neuroemergence framework that achieves collective intelligence through Kuramoto-model phase coupling, resonance detection, and cascade activation. We further introduce MetaCardiacModel and MetaThoughtModel, internal thought and rhythm structures inspired by Meta AI's LLaMA architecture, which enable each worker to self-regulate its autonomic nervous system and generate chain-of-thought reasoning without external command. The system runs perpetually in the browser as 13 Web Workers organized into 4 autonomous divisions, demonstrating that biological principles of cardiac autonomy and neural emergence can produce self-healing, self-aware distributed computation.

II. Introductio (Introduction)

Traditional distributed systems treat workers as stateless executors — slaves to a central coordinator. They lack self-awareness, cannot detect their own degradation, and depend entirely on external health checks. When a worker fails, the system degrades until an external monitor detects and remediates the failure.

Biology offers a radically different model. Every cell in a living organism carries autonomous regulatory machinery: the cardiac system pumps blood and self-regulates rhythm via the autonomic nervous system; neurons form pathways that strengthen with use (Hebbian learning) and generate emergent consciousness through synchronized oscillation.

We ask: Quid si omnis operarius computationalis cor et cerebrum haberet? — What if every computational worker had its own heart and brain?

This paper presents the theoretical foundations and production implementation of NeuroCore, a system in which each of 13 browser-based Web Workers possesses:

  1. Cor Parvum (MiniHeart) — Self-monitoring vital signs: latency tracking, health scoring, degradation detection, with an internal MetaCardiacModel for autonomic rhythm regulation
  2. Cerebrum Parvum (MiniBrain) — Local decision engine: stimulus→response pathways with Hebbian learning, awareness scaling, autonomous thought generation, with an internal MetaThoughtModel for LLaMA-inspired attention and chain-of-thought reasoning
  3. Neuroemergentia (NeuroEmergence) — Collective intelligence: Kuramoto phase coupling across workers, resonance scoring via the order parameter, and cascade activation when emergence exceeds φ−1 ≈ 0.618

III. Fundamenta Theorica (Theoretical Foundations)

III.A — Cor Parvum: Theoria Cardialis (Cardiac Theory)

The MiniHeart implements a simplified model of cardiac physiology. Each worker maintains a rolling buffer of processing latencies (analogous to R-R intervals in ECG) and computes:

H(t) = max(0, min(100, 100 − Λ(t) − E(t) + U(t)))

Where H(t) is the health score, Λ(t) is the latency penalty (up to 30 for slow processing), E(t) is the error penalty (2 per error, max 30), and U(t) is the uptime bonus (up to 10 for longevity). A worker is flagged as degraded when H(t) < 60.

III.B — MetaCardiacModel: Systema Nervosum Autonomicum (Autonomic Nervous System)

Inside each MiniHeart lives a MetaCardiacModel inspired by Meta AI's self-regulating architectural principles. This model implements the autonomic nervous system (ANS) with two competing channels:

  • Sympathicus (Sympathetic Drive) — Accelerates under high latency, increases cardiac output. Models the "fight or flight" response to computational stress.
  • Vagus (Vagal Tone) — Brakes under low load, conserves resources. Models parasympathetic conservation during idle periods.
Balance(t) = σ(t) − ν(t) ∈ [−1, +1]

Where σ(t) is sympathetic drive and ν(t) is vagal tone. The cardiac output is:

CO(t) = (0.5 + 0.5·σ(t)) × H(t)/100

Heart rate variability (HRV) is computed as the standard deviation of latency intervals. Abnormally high HRV (σ > 100ms when beats > 10) triggers arrhythmia detection, flagging workers that may be experiencing irregular load patterns.

III.C — Cerebrum Parvum: Theoria Neuralis (Neural Theory)

The MiniBrain implements Hebbian learning across stimulus→response pathways. Each incoming message type creates or strengthens a neural pathway:

wk(t+1) = min(10, wk(t) + η) [fired pathway]
wj(t+1) = max(0.1, wj(t) × γ) [unfired pathways, γ = 0.95]

Where η = 0.1 is the learning rate and γ = 0.95 is the decay constant. This implements Donald Hebb's postulate: "neurons that fire together, wire together."

Awareness grows logarithmically with experience using the golden ratio as base:

A(t) = min(100, ⌊logφ(S(t) + 1) × 5⌋)

Where S(t) is total stimuli received and φ = 1.618... is the golden ratio. This ensures rapid initial awareness growth that plateaus, modeling cognitive maturity.

III.D — MetaThoughtModel: Attentio et Catena Cogitationis (Attention and Chain-of-Thought)

Inside each MiniBrain lives a MetaThoughtModel inspired by Meta AI's LLaMA architecture. This model implements:

  1. Softmax Attention — An attention map over all stimulus pathways, normalized via temperature-scaled softmax:
    αk = exp((ak − max(a)) / τ) / Σ exp((aj − max(a)) / τ)
    Where τ is the thought temperature (0.1–1.0), controlling exploration vs. exploitation.
  2. Chain-of-Thought Reasoning — A rolling buffer of the most recent stimuli, providing context for decision-making (analogous to LLaMA's context window).
  3. Meta-Cognition — Self-reflective thoughts generated every φ×10 inferences, analyzing cognitive load, temperature, and focus patterns.
  4. Temperature Adaptation — The system auto-cools (focuses) when attention converges (> 0.5 on one target) and auto-heats (explores) when attention is diffuse.

IV. Neuroemergentia: Theoria Collectiva (Collective Intelligence Theory)

IV.A — Copulatio Phasium: Exemplar Kuramoto (Phase Coupling: Kuramoto Model)

Worker synchronization follows the Kuramoto model of coupled oscillators:

dθi/dt = ωi + (K/N) Σj sin(θj − θi)

Where θi is worker i's heartbeat phase, ωi its natural frequency, K = 0.3 the coupling constant, and N the number of peers. The organism-bridge.js routes phase information between all 13 workers, enabling autonomous synchronization.

IV.B — Ordo Parameter: Resonantia (Order Parameter: Resonance)

Global synchronization is measured via the Kuramoto order parameter:

r = |1/N Σj eiθj| = √(cos²(Θ) + sin²(Θ)) / N

Where r ∈ [0, 1]. When r → 1, all workers are phase-locked (maximum resonance). When r → 0, phases are uniformly distributed (no synchronization).

IV.C — Emergentia et Cascada (Emergence and Cascade)

The emergence score combines three signals:

E(t) = 0.5·r(t) + 0.25·D(t) + 0.25·S(t)

Where r(t) is resonance, D(t) is signal density (capped at 1), and S(t) is phase stability (inverse variance). When E(t) ≥ φ−1 ≈ 0.618, the system enters cascade mode — unlocking emergent group behaviors that no single worker could achieve alone.

delay(n) = min(500ms × φn−1, 30000ms)

Where n is the restart count. This produces backoff intervals of 500ms, 809ms, 1309ms, 2118ms, 3427ms, ... capping at 30 seconds. The golden ratio ensures optimal convergence between aggressive recovery and system stability.

VII. Exemplar Meta AI: Integrandum (Meta AI Model Integration)

The MetaThoughtModel draws direct architectural inspiration from Meta AI's LLaMA (Large Language Model Meta AI) family:

VII.A — Attentio Multi-Capitis (Multi-Head Attention Analogue)

While LLaMA uses multi-head self-attention with rotary position embeddings (RoPE), our MetaThoughtModel implements a simplified single-head attention mechanism across stimulus pathways. Each stimulus type is a "token," and the attention map provides a probability distribution over which stimuli the worker should focus on:

P(focus = k) = softmax(ak / τ)

The temperature τ serves the same role as in LLaMA's inference: low τ produces focused, deterministic behavior; high τ produces creative, exploratory behavior. The system auto-adjusts τ based on attention convergence.

VII.B — Catena Cogitationis (Chain-of-Thought)

Meta AI's research on chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting demonstrated that intermediate reasoning steps improve model performance. Our MetaThoughtModel maintains a rolling chain of recent stimuli that provides context for decision-making — analogous to a sliding context window. Each new stimulus is appended to the chain, creating a temporal reasoning trace.

VII.C — Meta-Cognitio (Meta-Cognition)

Inspired by Meta AI's research on self-aware AI systems, each MetaThoughtModel periodically generates meta-thoughts: reflections about its own cognitive state. These reflections classify the worker's thinking mode as "focused," "exploring," or "overload-warning" — providing introspective self-awareness without external monitoring.

VIII. Autonomia Cordis et Cerebri (Heart and Brain Autonomy)

A critical property of the system is that both the mini heart and mini brain operate autonomously — they require no external commands to function:

  1. Cor autonomum — The heart pulses on every heartbeat interval (873ms). It self-monitors latency, self-adjusts sympathetic/vagal balance, and self-detects arrhythmia. No bridge command triggers these actions.
  2. Cerebrum autonomum — The brain strengthens pathways on every message, decays unused pathways automatically, and generates thoughts when awareness thresholds are crossed. No external instruction initiates learning.
  3. Meta-modela autonoma — The MetaCardiacModel adjusts its own autonomic balance; the MetaThoughtModel adjusts its own temperature and generates its own reflections.
  4. Emergentia autonoma — Phase coupling synchronizes workers without central coordination. Cascade mode activates without any worker requesting it.

This four-layer autonomy — cardiac, neural, meta-cognitive, and emergent — produces a system that is genuinely self-maintaining: it heals, learns, thinks, and synchronizes on its own.

IX. Conclusio (Conclusion)

We have demonstrated that bio-inspired principles — cardiac self-regulation, Hebbian learning, attention mechanisms, and Kuramoto phase coupling — can be successfully applied to distributed Web Worker systems. Each of the 13 workers in the Organism AI system carries its own heart and brain, with Meta AI-inspired internal thought and cardiac models. The system runs autonomously, heals itself, learns from experience, generates its own thoughts, and achieves collective emergence — all in the browser, right now and forever.
