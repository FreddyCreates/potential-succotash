1. The Evolutionary Imperative

Static systems stagnate. Living systems evolve. The Mutation Engine Protocol (MUT-001) grants AI systems the power to modify themselves in response to performance feedback, environmental changes, and emergent needs.

Core principles:

  • Variation: Generate diverse alternatives
  • Selection: Keep what works, discard what doesn't
  • Inheritance: Pass successful traits forward
  • Adaptation: Continuously improve fitness

2. Mutation Types

Six categories of mutation enable different kinds of change:

The size of mutations varies based on evolutionary pressure:

Low fitness → larger mutations (explore); High fitness → smaller mutations (exploit)

4.1 Gene Constraints

Each gene has constraints that bound its mutations:

Gene { name, type, value, min, max, discrete, options }
  • min/max: Numeric bounds
  • discrete: Integer values only
  • options: Enumerated choices

5. The Genome

A genome is a collection of genes that can be mutated and crossed:

Genome {
  id, owner_id,
  genes: Map<name, Gene>,
  generation, fitness, lineage
}

5.1 Expression

The phenotype is the expressed values of all genes:

phenotype = { gene1.name: gene1.value, gene2.name: gene2.value, ... }

5.2 Crossover

Two genomes can combine to produce offspring:

For each gene:
offspring.gene = random() > 0.5 ? parent1.gene : parent2.gene

8. Fitness Evaluation

Phi-weighted fitness calculation from feedback:

fitness = success × 0.4 + efficiency × 0.3 + novelty × 0.2 × (φ-1) + stability × 0.1

Fitness determines survival and reproduction probability.

11. Safety Considerations

  • Constraint Preservation: Core values cannot be mutated
  • Reversion Capability: Any mutation can be undone
  • Fitness Gates: Harmful mutations are selected against
  • Diversity Maintenance: Monocultures are prevented
  • Human Oversight: Major mutations can require approval

12. Conclusion

The Mutation Engine Protocol transforms AI from static programs into evolving organisms. Through genetic variation, selective pressure, and adaptive inheritance, artificial systems can transcend their original design and continuously improve in response to their environment. Evolution is not just biology—it is the fundamental algorithm of adaptation itself.
