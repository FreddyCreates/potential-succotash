A framework for token supply, inflation control, reserve management, and shared fiscal decision making

I. Abstract

Federated intelligence networks need economic rules that reward contribution without inviting capture. This paper outlines a constitutional economics for distributed AI systems, covering token issuance, inflation control, treasury management, grant allocation, and emergency reserves under phi-weighted governance.

II. Introduction

Economic coordination becomes unavoidable once nodes share compute, data stewardship, and long-term protocol maintenance. Without fiscal rules, federation devolves into opportunism or hidden subsidy.

A constitutional treasury transforms shared value into transparent public finance for the network.

III. Token Supply Design

Supply policy should reward verified contribution while resisting runaway inflation. We therefore distinguish productive issuance, maintenance issuance, and emergency issuance, each with separate governance thresholds.

Token design is not merely monetary. It encodes what the network chooses to value.

NetSupply(t) = Minted(t) − Burned(t) − Decayed(t)

IV. Inflation Control

Inflation is managed by constraining issuance to verified growth in network utility and by applying decay to dormant balances when chartered. The goal is adaptive liquidity rather than maximal scarcity.

Phi-weighted policy allows gradual changes that remain legible to members.

V. Treasury Management

A federated treasury must balance reserves, operating grants, and contingency pools. Shared reserves provide continuity for maintenance, research, and incident response.

Treasury policy is legitimate only when disbursements are fully auditable and bound to declared purposes.

  • Core reserves fund constitutional infrastructure.
  • Research pools fund long-horizon experimentation.
  • Emergency reserves fund recovery after security or operational shocks.

VI. Budget Governance

Budgeting converts strategic priorities into actual allocations. Phi-quorum voting is useful here because treasury capture is among the highest-risk federation failures.

Higher thresholds for fiscal action protect the commons from impulsive depletion.

VII. Failure Modes

The main economic failure modes are hidden concentration, underfunded maintenance, and emergency overreach. Each failure can be mitigated by better disclosure and stronger constitutional controls.

Economic design must therefore work in concert with federation law, not outside it.

VIII. Conclusion

Federated intelligence needs public finance, not improvised accounting. A chartered treasury and disciplined token economics let the network invest, respond to crisis, and preserve legitimacy over time.

Canonical use: this paper supplies the economic logic behind treasury thresholds, admission incentives, and reserve discipline for federated intelligence networks.
